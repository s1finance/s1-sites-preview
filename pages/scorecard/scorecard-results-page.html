Cash Flow Visibility & Forecasting

3 / 12

You're making decisions based on what's in the bank account right now — which means you're always reacting, never anticipating. Most owners in this position have experienced at least one cash surprise in the past year that didn't need to happen.

Here's the thing: you're probably profitable. Cash flow problems and profitability problems aren't the same thing — but without visibility, they feel identical. A structured cash forecast would separate the two and give you breathing room you didn't know you had.

One thing to consider

A 13-week rolling cash forecast — updated weekly — is the single highest-impact financial tool for businesses at your stage. It takes the guesswork out of "can I afford this?"

Financial Reporting & Close Process

7 / 12

You have reporting, but there's a lag or a gap. Maybe your books close two or three weeks after month-end. Maybe you get a P&L but not much else. Maybe the numbers are there but you're not entirely sure they're right.

That gap between "I have numbers" and "I trust these numbers enough to make a decision today" is where most growing businesses get stuck. It's not that you need more reports — you need the right reports, faster, with someone accountable for their accuracy.

One thing to consider

The goal isn't just faster reporting — it's reporting you can act on within the first week of every month.

Budgeting, Planning & KPI Tracking

6 / 12

You have pieces of the planning puzzle — maybe a budget, maybe some KPIs, maybe an informal sense of what's profitable. But it's not integrated into a system that tells you whether you're on track and, if not, what to do about it.

The missing link is usually the review rhythm. A budget without monthly variance analysis is just a document. KPIs without thresholds and action plans are just numbers. The planning infrastructure is there — it just needs to become a decision-making loop rather than a reference document.

One thing to consider

Set a recurring monthly meeting — even 30 minutes — to review actuals vs. budget and your top 5 KPIs. The discipline of the review matters more than the perfection of the plan.

Financial Systems & Infrastructure

Your systems are solid — automated, documented, and designed to run without depending on any single person. This is a sign of operational maturity that directly impacts your business's scalability and value.

At this level, the opportunity is continuous improvement: staying current with tools and integrations, finding the last 5–10% of manual work, and making sure your technology is earning its license fees in real productivity gains.

Financial Team & Advisor Alignment

6 / 12

You have advisors, and you use them — but it's probably more ad-hoc than structured. You call when there's a question, you meet when there's a need, and the relationship works well enough. But there's no consistent rhythm and no one person who sees the full financial picture.

The difference between "advisor I call sometimes" and "advisor who knows my business" is proactive insight. One answers questions. The other sees problems coming and brings solutions before you ask.

One thing to consider

A quarterly financial strategy meeting — even 60 minutes — with someone who knows your numbers, your goals, and your industry can surface opportunities you'd otherwise miss.
